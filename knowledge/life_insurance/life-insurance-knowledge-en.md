## 2. Definition and Scope of Life Insurance

**Life insurance (Asuransi Jiwa)** provides payments to the policyholder, insured, or another entitled party in the event that:
- The insured **dies**, or
- The insured **survives** to a specified date, or
- Another specified event occurs as agreed in the contract, with benefits of a pre-determined amount and/or based on investment returns.

(Based on UU No. 40/2014 Article 1 and 2)

## 3. Life Insurance Product Types in Indonesia

### 3.1 Term Life Insurance (Asuransi Jiwa Berjangka)
- Pays a **death benefit only** if the insured dies within the specified term (1, 5, 10, 20 years, or to a specified age).
- No cash value accumulation.
- Lowest premium among all life insurance types.
- Suitable for pure protection (e.g., mortgage protection).

### 3.2 Whole Life Insurance (Asuransi Jiwa Seumur Hidup)
- Pays death benefit for the **entire lifetime** of the insured.
- Accumulates cash value that the policyholder may access.
- Can serve as a fixed-return investment instrument.

### 3.3 Endowment Insurance (Asuransi Jiwa Dwiguna)
- Dual benefit: death benefit if insured dies within the term AND maturity benefit if insured survives to term end.
- Policy terms: 5, 10, 15, 20, or 30 years.
- Guaranteed returns; suitable for education funding or retirement planning.

### 3.4 Unit-Linked Insurance / PAYDI (Investment-Linked)
- Combines life protection and investment value that fluctuates with market performance.
- Investment value depends on the sub-fund(s) selected by the policyholder.
- Most complex product; requires an appointed company actuary.
- Strictly regulated by OJK under POJK 8/2024 (separate recording of PAYDI assets and liabilities).

### 3.5 Additional Lines Sold by Life Insurance Companies
- **Health Insurance**: Hospitalization, outpatient, critical illness coverage.
- **Personal Accident Insurance**: Death, disability, medical costs from accidents.
- **Credit Life Insurance (Jiwa Kredit)**: Covers outstanding loan balance if borrower dies or becomes disabled.
- **Annuity (Anuitas)**: Periodic payments to policyholder, typically used for pension programs.

## 4. Life Insurance Underwriting Principles

### 4.1 Purpose of Underwriting
To assess the eligibility of prospective insured before policy issuance, including:
- Assessing mortality and morbidity risk
- Ensuring the sum insured is proportionate to the insured's financial circumstances
- Preventing anti-selection and moral hazard
- AML/KYC compliance

### 4.2 Three Subjects of Life Insurance Underwriting Assessment
1. **Policyholder (Pemegang Polis)**: Must have a valid insurable interest relationship with the insured (parent-child, spouse, employer-employee, etc.). Domicile, age, and legal identity must be verified.
2. **Insured (Tertanggung)**: The person on whose life the coverage is based. Assessment covers age, gender, occupation, health, hobbies, lifestyle, and income.
3. **Beneficiary (Penerima Manfaat)**: Must have a clear and reasonable insurable interest in the insured. Can be an individual, trustee, or institution.

### 4.3 Key Underwriting Factors

| Factor | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| Age | Older age → higher mortality risk → higher premium |
| Gender | Males and females have different mortality rates |
| Health condition | Medical history, blood pressure, diabetes, cancer, etc. |
| Occupation | High-risk occupations (mining, hazardous materials) → extra premium |
| Dangerous hobbies/sports | Motor racing, diving, paragliding → extra premium or exclusion |
| Smoking/tobacco use | Non-smokers receive lower premiums |
| Family health history | Diabetes, heart disease, cancer in family = risk factor |
| Sum assured | Must be proportionate to insured's income and financial position |
| Existing policies | Anti-selection risk if insured holds many policies |

### 4.4 Underwriting Decisions
- **Accept Standard**: Risk meets standard criteria, normal premium.
- **Accept Substandard**: Higher risk, extra premium and/or specific exclusions.
- **Postpone**: Decision deferred pending additional information.
- **Decline**: Risk too high or does not meet insurer's criteria.

## 5. Key Financial Metrics for Life Insurance Analysis

### 5.1 Premium Income (Pendapatan Premi)
```
Gross Premium = Total premiums received before reinsurance
Net Premium = Gross Premium − Ceded Reinsurance Premium
```
Premium growth is the primary indicator of business expansion.

### 5.2 Claims and Benefits Expense (Beban Klaim dan Manfaat)
Includes:
- Death claims
- Critical illness claims
- Health claims
- Maturity benefits (endowment payouts)
- Surrender value paid (policy cancellations)
- Accident claims

### 5.3 Loss Ratio / Claims Ratio
```
Loss Ratio = Claims and Benefits Expense / Net Premium Income × 100%
```
Life insurance loss ratios tend to be more stable than general insurance due to the long-term nature of products.

### 5.4 Risk Based Capital (RBC)
- OJK minimum: **120%**
- RBC < 120%: Company must inject additional capital; OJK may intervene.

### 5.5 Technical Reserves (Cadangan Teknis)
Key liability components in life insurance balance sheet:
- **Premium Reserve**: Funds set aside to pay future claims on in-force policies.
- **IBNR (Incurred But Not Reported)**: Estimated claims that occurred but not yet reported.
- **Surrender Value Liability**: Obligation to policyholders if they cancel their policies.
- **PAYDI/Unit-Link Investment Funds**: Assets managed on behalf of unit-linked policyholders.

### 5.6 Investment Income (Hasil Investasi)
Life insurance companies are heavily dependent on investment income because:
- Premium funds are collected long before claims fall due (especially long-term products)
- Common asset allocation: time deposits, government bonds, mutual funds, equities

Key investment indicators:
- Return on Investment (ROI)
- Government bond yield trends (benchmark)
- Investment portfolio composition by instrument

### 5.7 Persistency Rate
Percentage of policies still in force after a specified period (typically 13 months). Indicator of sales quality and customer satisfaction.

### 5.8 Solvency and Equity
```
Equity = Total Assets − Total Liabilities
```
Equity growth reflects the company's capacity to accumulate capital.

## 6. Life Insurance Financial Statement: Key Line Items

### 6.1 Income Statement
| Line Item | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Gross Premium Income | Total premiums before reinsurance |
| Reinsurance Premium | Premiums ceded to reinsurers |
| Net Premium Income | Gross − Reinsurance |
| Investment Income | Interest, dividends, investment gains |
| Claims and Benefits Expense | All claim and benefit payments |
| Commission Expense | Agent and broker commissions |
| Operating Expense | Salaries, rent, IT, etc. |
| Underwriting Result | Revenue − Claims − Commissions − Operations |
| Net Profit / (Loss) | After tax |

### 6.2 Balance Sheet
| Assets | Liabilities |
|--------|-------------|
| Investment Assets (deposits, bonds, stocks, mutual funds) | Premium Reserve |
| Non-Investment Assets (premium receivables, reinsurance claims receivable) | Claims Reserve (IBNR) |
| Cash and Cash Equivalents | Claims Payable |
| Property/Fixed Assets | PAYDI Investment Funds |
| | Other Liabilities |
| | Equity |

## 7. Indonesian Life Insurance Market Context

### 7.1 Market Structure (2022–2023)
- Number of life insurance companies: ~59–61
  - State-owned & domestic private: ~36–37
  - Joint venture: ~23–24
- Total assets (end of 2022): ~IDR 585 trillion (+3% CAGR over 5 years)
- Premium income (2022): ~IDR 169.95 trillion
- Claims & benefits (2022): ~IDR 157.53 trillion (CAGR: −2.4% over 5 years)

### 7.2 Key BUMN Life Insurers
- PT Asuransi Jiwa IFG (member of IFG Group)
- PT Jiwasraya (state-owned; major restructuring)
- PT Taspen

### 7.3 Product Dominance
- PAYDI (Unit-Link) dominates portfolio of many large life insurers
- Credit life (bancassurance channel) has grown strongly
- Group health insurance has become a growth driver post-pandemic

### 7.4 Distribution Channels
- Insurance agents (largest distribution channel)
- Bancassurance (bank partners)
- Telemarketing
- Digital/online

### 7.5 Industry Challenges
- COVID-19 pandemic: surge in health and life claims 2020–2022
- Major insolvency cases (Jiwasraya, AJB Bumiputera) eroding public trust
- Low penetration and density vs. ASEAN peers
- PAYDI complexity → high volume of consumer complaints
- OJK strengthening PAYDI regulations via POJK 8/2024

### 7.6 Reinsurance in Life Insurance
- Cession rate in life insurance much lower than general insurance (~3% of gross premium on average)
- Life reinsurance primarily covers mortality and disability risk
- Many joint-venture life insurers reinsure with their foreign parent groups

## 9. Analysis Framework

### 9.1 Key Monthly Questions
1. Is premium income growing or declining vs. prior month / prior year?
2. Are claims and benefits increasing proportionally?
3. How is the investment income trend? Is ROI declining?
4. Is there a significant increase in surrender value payments? (Indicator of policy lapses)
5. Is RBC still above 120%?
6. What is the product portfolio composition? (PAYDI vs. non-PAYDI)

### 9.2 Red Flags
- Sharp decline in premium income → potential distribution or reputational issues
- Claims spike → possible epidemic/pandemic trigger or abnormal claims
- Sharply rising surrender values → policyholder confidence crisis
- Investment ROI below 4% → suboptimal asset management
- RBC approaching 120% → capital adequacy concern
- Insufficient technical reserves → long-term solvency risk
- Increasing consumer complaints → product quality or service issues

### 9.3 Positive Signals
- Consistent premium growth with controlled claims
- High persistency rate (policyholders retaining policies)
- Good product diversification
- Stable ROI above benchmark
- RBC well above 120%
- Successful bancassurance or digital channel expansion

## 10. Commentary Style Guide

### 10.1 Language and Tone
- Output language: **Bahasa Indonesia** — formal, professional, precise
- Include specific numbers with period comparisons (MoM, YoY)
- Provide macroeconomic context where relevant (interest rates, medical inflation)
- Avoid overly technical jargon without explanation

### 10.2 Example of Good Commentary
> "PT Asuransi Jiwa ABC mencatat pendapatan premi bruto sebesar Rp 12,3 triliun pada Januari–April 2026, tumbuh 6,2% dibandingkan periode yang sama tahun lalu. Produk PAYDI tetap mendominasi dengan kontribusi 68% dari total premi. Beban klaim dan manfaat meningkat 9,4% menjadi Rp 9,8 triliun, terutama didorong oleh kenaikan klaim kesehatan dan peningkatan surrender value. Hasil investasi tercatat Rp 2,1 triliun, sedikit di bawah tahun lalu (Rp 2,3 triliun), mencerminkan tekanan pada yield obligasi. RBC tetap terjaga di 148%, masih memadai meski sedikit menurun dari 155% akhir 2025."

### 10.3 What to Avoid
- ❌ Mixing figures from different companies without labeling
- ❌ Stating conclusions without supporting numbers
- ❌ Citing figures not present in the source data files
- ❌ Inferring trends from insufficient historical data

## 11. Anti-Hallucination Instructions

1. **Use only data from provided files** in the data/ folder.
2. **Cite the source file name and sheet/tab** when referencing any number.
3. **If data is unavailable**, state "data not available for this period."
4. **Verify internal consistency** of figures (gross premium − reinsurance = net premium, etc.).
5. **Do not assume or estimate** figures that are missing or unreadable.
6. **Flag discrepancies** between sources rather than silently choosing one.
