# Indonesian Insurance Glossary
## Shared Reference for All Analysis Agents

> **Primary Sources:** AAJI Glossary (aaji.or.id), UU No. 40/2014, POJK 23/2023, POJK 8/2024, CGI 001 Indonesia Re, Eco Bulletin Reinsurance 101, AAJI Underwriting Guidelines, SKKNI 2024.
>
> **Usage Note:** This glossary is a shared reference for all insurance analysis agents. Refer here when encountering unfamiliar terms. All Indonesian terms are preserved alongside English equivalents.
>
> **Anti-hallucination:** Do not redefine terms outside this glossary. If a term is absent, state that verification from official sources is required.

---

## A

**Acceptance** *(Akseptasi)*
Agreement by an insurance company to accept an application from a prospective policyholder to cover a risk.

**Actuary** *(Aktuaria)*
A professional using statistics and probability to analyze financial risk, set premiums, and project long-term insurance obligations.

**Agent (Insurance)** *(Agen Asuransi)*
Representative of an insurance company who solicits, collects, and services policyholders. Notice to an agent is equivalent to notice to the company.

**Average Condition** *(Kondisi Average)*
A policy provision that applies when the insured sum is less than the actual value at risk. The insurer only pays a proportional share of the loss (underinsurance penalty). Formula: (Sum Insured / Value at Risk) × Loss.

---

## B

**Beneficiary** *(Penerima Manfaat)*
The person or entity designated in a policy to receive the benefit or sum insured.

**Broker (Insurance)** *(Broker Asuransi)*
An independent intermediary representing the insured's interests in placing insurance.

**Broker (Reinsurance)** *(Broker Reasuransi)*
An intermediary connecting insurance companies with reinsurers in placing reinsurance.

---

## C

**Cash Value** *(Nilai Tunai)*
The savings element that accumulates in a whole life or unit-linked policy, which can be surrendered before policy expiry.

**Ceding Company / Cedent** *(Ceding Company)*
The insurance company that transfers (cedes) a portion of its risk to a reinsurer.

**Cession** *(Sesi)*
The portion of the sum insured transferred to the reinsurer.

**Claim** *(Klaim)*
A demand by the insured/policyholder for payment from the insurer as stipulated in the policy.

**Claims Reserve** *(Cadangan Klaim)*
Funds set aside to pay claims that have occurred but not yet been settled.

**Co-Insurance** *(Ko-Asuransi)*
The sharing of a risk between two or more insurance companies, each bearing a defined proportion of risk, premium, and loss.

**Combined Operating Ratio (COR)**
Comprehensive underwriting performance measure: COR = Loss Ratio + Commission Ratio + Expense Ratio. Below 100% = underwriting profit.

**Contribution** *(Kontribusi)*
A legal principle that when two or more policies cover the same risk, losses are shared proportionally. Each insurer pays its rateable share.

---

## D

**Death Benefit / Sum Insured** *(Santunan Kematian / Uang Pertanggungan)*
The amount paid by a life insurer to the beneficiary upon the insured's death during the coverage period.

**Deductible** *(Deductible)*
A large amount borne by the insured before the insurer pays; reduces premium accordingly.

**Dual Insurance** *(Asuransi Rangkap)*
A situation where two or more policies are in force covering the same interest against the same risk.

---

## E

**Earned Loss Ratio (ELR)**
Claims incurred during a specific period (January–December) relative to earned premium in the same period.

**Endowment Insurance** *(Asuransi Jiwa Dwiguna)*
Life insurance that pays the sum insured on death during the term, or to the policyholder if still alive at the end of the term.

**Equity** *(Ekuitas)*
The value belonging to shareholders after all liabilities are met. A key solvency and stability indicator.

**Excess** *(Excess)*
A small amount deducted from each claim, borne by the insured; the insurer pays the remainder.

---

## F

**Facultative Reinsurance** *(Reasuransi Fakultatif)*
Reinsurance arranged for a single, specific risk on a case-by-case basis.

**Frequency and Severity** *(Frekuensi dan Severity)*
Two dimensions for assessing risk: how often a loss occurs (frequency) and how large it is when it does (severity). High frequency–low severity risks are more predictable; low frequency–high severity risks require reinsurance.

---

## G

**General Insurance** *(Asuransi Umum / Asuransi Kerugian)*
Insurance covering property damage or loss, and legal liability to third parties. Also called non-life insurance.

**Guarantee Fund** *(Dana Jaminan)*
Funds deposited by an insurance/reinsurance company with OJK as security for its ability to meet obligations.

---

## H

**Hazard** *(Hazard)*
Factors influencing the likelihood or magnitude of a peril.
- **Physical Hazard:** Physical characteristics of the insured object (construction, age, location, security).
- **Moral Hazard:** Behavior or attitude of the insured that increases risk (carelessness, dishonesty).

---

## I

**IBNR (Incurred But Not Reported)**
Claims that have occurred but have not yet been reported to the insurer. Must be reserved in financial statements.

**Indemnity** *(Ganti Rugi)*
Financial compensation restoring the insured to their pre-loss financial position. The insured must not profit from a claim (fundamental non-life insurance principle).

**Insurable Interest** *(Kepentingan yang Dapat Diasuransikan)*
A legally recognized financial relationship between the insured and the subject matter of insurance. A mandatory requirement for a valid insurance contract.

**Insurance** *(Asuransi)*
An agreement between an insurer and a policyholder wherein the insurer accepts premiums in exchange for compensating losses or paying defined benefits. (UU No. 40/2014)

---

## K

**KPPE (Kelompok Perusahaan Perasuransian Ekuitas)**
OJK classification of insurance companies based on minimum equity under POJK 23/2023:
- KPPE 1: Lower equity; limited to simple insurance products.
- KPPE 2: Higher equity; authorized for all insurance activities.

---

## L

**Lapse** *(Lapse)*
Termination of a policy due to non-payment of premium within the grace period.

**Lapse Ratio** *(Lapse Ratio)*
The percentage of lapsed policies relative to total in-force policies. A key portfolio quality indicator in life insurance.

**Liability Insurance** *(Asuransi Tanggung Gugat)*
Insurance covering legal liability to pay damages and costs to third parties for injury, death, or property damage caused by the insured.

**Life Insurance** *(Asuransi Jiwa)*
Insurance protecting against the financial risk of death or survival, providing financial security for dependents.

**Line** *(Line)*
The proportion accepted by a reinsurer; the ceding company's retention is the base "one line."

**Loss Ratio** — *see Claim Ratio*

---

## M

**Material Fact** *(Fakta Material)*
Any information that would influence an underwriter's decision on premium or risk acceptance.

**Minimum Equity** *(Ekuitas Minimum)*
OJK's minimum capital requirement for insurance/reinsurance companies under POJK 23/2023.

---

## O

**OJK (Otoritas Jasa Keuangan)**
Indonesia's Financial Services Authority — the regulatory body overseeing the entire financial services sector, including insurance.

**Outstanding Loss Ratio (OLR)**
Claims incurred relative to booked premiums (premiums agreed but possibly not yet paid).

---

## P

**Peril** *(Peril)*
An event or cause that can lead to loss (fire, flood, theft, accident).

**Policy** *(Polis)*
The written document evidencing the insurance contract; not the contract itself, but proof of it.

**Premium** *(Premi)*
The amount paid by the policyholder to the insurer in exchange for coverage.

**Premium Reserve** *(Cadangan Premi)*
Funds reserved from received premiums to meet future obligations under in-force policies.

**Proximate Cause** *(Penyebab Terdekat)*
The dominant and most directly related cause of a loss; the basis for determining whether a claim is payable.

---

## R

**RBC (Risk-Based Capital)**
Capital adequacy measure based on risk profile. OJK minimum: 120%. Formula: Available Capital / Minimum Required Capital × 100%.

**Reinsurance** *(Reasuransi)*
An agreement where an insurer transfers part of its risk to a reinsurer in exchange for part of the premium. "Insurance for insurers."

**Reinsurer** *(Reasuradur)*
A company accepting reinsurance business from insurers, bearing a share of the insured risks.

**Retention** *(Retensi)*
The portion of risk retained by the insurer and not ceded to a reinsurer.

**Retrocession** *(Retrosesi)*
The process by which a reinsurer transfers part of its assumed risk to another reinsurer.

**Retrocessionaire** *(Retrosesioner)*
A reinsurer that accepts retroceded business from another reinsurer.

**RIPLAY (Ringkasan Informasi Produk dan/atau Layanan)**
Mandatory product summary document provided to prospective customers before purchase, covering features, benefits, risks, costs, and terms.

**Risk** *(Risiko)*
Uncertainty about future loss.
- **Pure Risk:** Can only result in loss or no change; insurable.
- **Speculative Risk:** Can result in profit or loss; not insurable.
- **Fundamental Risk:** Widespread, catastrophic impact (war, major disaster); generally not insurable.
- **Particular Risk:** Limited impact on individuals or specific groups; generally insurable.

---

## S

**Self-Insurance** *(Asuransi Sendiri)*
The practice of retaining risk internally rather than transferring it to an insurer.

**Sharia Insurance** *(Asuransi Syariah / Takaful)*
A cooperative risk-sharing arrangement based on Islamic principles.

**Solvency** *(Solvabilitas)*
The ability of an insurance company to meet all its obligations to policyholders. Measured by the RBC ratio (minimum 120%).

**Subrogation** *(Subrogasi)*
The insurer's right to take over the insured's right to recover from a liable third party after paying the claim.

**Sum Insured** *(Nilai Pertanggungan / Uang Pertanggungan)*
The maximum amount payable by the insurer per claim event.

---

## T

**Technical Reserve** *(Cadangan Teknis)*
The total mandatory reserves formed to ensure the company can fulfill obligations to policyholders. Includes premium reserves, claims reserves, and others.

**Treaty Reinsurance** *(Reasuransi Treaty)*
An automatic reinsurance agreement covering a portfolio of risks meeting agreed criteria, without individual risk negotiation.

---

## U

**Underinsurance** *(Underinsurance)*
A situation where the sum insured is less than the actual value at risk, resulting in proportionally reduced claim payments.

**Underwriter** *(Underwriter)*
A professional who assesses risks and decides on acceptance terms, conditions, and premiums.

**Utmost Good Faith** *(Itikad Baik Tertinggi)*
The obligation of all parties to voluntarily and fully disclose all material facts accurately during insurance contract negotiation. Breach may void the policy.

---

## W

**Warranty** *(Warranty / Jaminan Polis)*
A promise by the insured regarding specific facts or conditions; must be strictly complied with. Breach may void a claim.

**Whole Life Insurance** *(Asuransi Jiwa Seumur Hidup)*
Life insurance covering the insured for their entire life; pays the death benefit whenever death occurs.
