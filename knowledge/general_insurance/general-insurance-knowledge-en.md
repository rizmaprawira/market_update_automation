## 2. Definition and Scope of General Insurance

**General insurance (Asuransi Umum)** provides indemnity to policyholders/insured parties for:
- Property damage and loss
- Costs arising from uncertain events
- Loss of profit
- Legal liability to third parties

Under UU No. 40/2014 Article 2, general insurance companies may only operate **General Insurance Business** (including health and personal accident lines) and may conduct **Reinsurance** for risks of other general insurance companies.

## 3. Lines of Business in Indonesian General Insurance

### 3.1 Property Insurance
- **Fire Insurance**: Covers buildings, contents, and stock against fire, lightning, explosion, smoke.
- **All Risks Property**: Covers all perils except explicitly excluded.
- **Engineering Insurance**: Covers machinery breakdown, construction all risks, erection all risks.
- **Theft Insurance**: Covers loss due to theft involving force or violence.

### 3.2 Motor Vehicle Insurance (Asuransi Kendaraan Bermotor)
- Covers damage to vehicles and/or third-party legal liability.
- Types: Comprehensive, Total Loss Only (TLO), Third Party Only.
- Key underwriting factors: driver age, vehicle type/age/value, usage (personal/commercial), geographic area, claims history.

### 3.3 Marine Insurance
- **Hull**: Covers vessel, machinery, equipment.
- **Cargo**: Covers goods being transported.
- **Freight**: Covers freight charges.

### 3.4 Aviation Insurance
- Covers aircraft damage and legal liability to third parties and passengers.
- High severity/low frequency → most aviation risks are facultatively reinsured.

### 3.5 Liability Insurance (Asuransi Tanggung Gugat)
- **Public Liability**: Legal liability for bodily injury or property damage to third parties.
- **Products Liability**: Liability for injuries caused by products manufactured/sold.
- **Professional Indemnity**: Liability for financial loss to clients due to professional negligence.
- **Employers' Liability**: Liability to employees for work-related injury or illness.

### 3.6 Personal Accident and Health Insurance
- Covers death, disability, or medical costs from accidents.
- Health insurance covers medical treatment costs (indemnity) or cash benefits (benefit basis).

### 3.7 Credit and Surety Insurance
- Credit Insurance: Guarantees against debtor's failure to meet financial obligations.
- Suretyship: Guarantees principal's ability to perform contract obligations to the obligee.

### 3.8 Business Interruption Insurance (BI)
- Covers loss of gross profit due to business stoppage caused by an insured peril.
- Maximum indemnity period must be defined in policy.

## 4. Core Insurance Principles

### 4.1 Insurable Interest
Must exist at both policy inception and at the time of loss (for non-marine general insurance).

### 4.2 Utmost Good Faith
Both parties must voluntarily disclose all material facts. Breach may render the policy void ab initio.

### 4.3 Indemnity
Compensation restores the insured to the same financial position as before the loss.

### 4.4 Proximate Cause
The dominant cause in the chain of events producing a loss determines whether the claim is covered.

### 4.5 Subrogation
After paying a claim, the insurer acquires the insured's right to recover from the responsible third party.

### 4.6 Contribution
Where two or more policies cover the same risk, each pays a rateable proportion of the loss.

## 5. Key Financial Metrics for General Insurance Analysis

### 5.1 Loss Ratio (Rasio Klaim)
```
Loss Ratio = Claims Incurred / Net Premium Earned × 100%
```
- Healthy benchmark: 60–70%
- > 80%: significant underwriting pressure
- > 100%: technical underwriting loss on that line

### 5.2 Commission Ratio
```
Commission Ratio = Commission Expense / Gross Written Premium × 100%
```

### 5.3 Expense Ratio
```
Expense Ratio = Operating Expense / Net Premium × 100%
```

### 5.4 Combined Operating Ratio (COR)
```
COR = Loss Ratio + Commission Ratio + Expense Ratio
```
- COR < 100%: Underwriting profit
- COR > 100%: Underwriting loss

### 5.5 Underwriting Result
```
Underwriting Result = Net Premium − Claims − Commissions − Operating Expenses
```

### 5.6 Net Written Premium (NWP)
```
NWP = Gross Written Premium (GWP) − Ceded Reinsurance Premium
```

### 5.7 Risk Based Capital (RBC)
- OJK minimum: **120%**
- RBC < 120%: Company must inject capital; OJK may intervene.

### 5.8 Retention Rate
```
Retention Rate = NWP / GWP × 100%
```

### 5.9 Cession Rate
```
Cession Rate = Ceded Reinsurance Premium / GWP × 100%
```

### 5.10 Investment Income
Income from placement of invested assets. Critical for companies with marginal underwriting results.

## 6. General Insurance Underwriting Process

### 6.1 Underwriting Steps
1. Risk Identification: Proposal form, risk survey, client meetings.
2. Risk Analysis: Assessment of physical and moral hazard.
3. Decision: Accept (standard/substandard), decline, or accept with conditions.
4. Premium Setting: Based on applicable rates adjusted for risk profile.

### 6.2 Underwriting Factors by Line

**Fire/Property:**
- Building construction type, occupancy, stored goods
- Protection measures (alarms, sprinklers, security)
- Location (flood/earthquake/high-crime zone)
- Claims history

**Motor:**
- Driver age and experience, vehicle type/age/value
- Usage (personal/commercial/fleet)
- Geographic area, vehicle modifications, claims history

**Marine Cargo:**
- Type of goods, packing, transit route, conveyance type

**Liability:**
- Nature of business, number of employees, revenue, claims history

## 7. Indonesian General Insurance Market Context

### 7.1 Market Structure (2022–2023)
- Number of general insurance companies: ~77–80
- Total general insurance premium (2022): ~IDR 78 trillion (+7% CAGR)
- Claims grew at 8.3% CAGR (2018–2022)
- Indonesia insurance penetration: ~2.7% of GDP

### 7.2 Industry Trends
- Motor vehicle and property remain dominant lines
- Growing digital distribution
- OJK Roadmap 2023–2027: consolidation, capital strengthening, GRC, digital transformation

## 9. Analysis Framework

### 9.1 Key Monthly Questions
1. Is gross premium growing or contracting vs. prior month/year?
2. Is loss ratio increasing? Which line is driving it?
3. Is COR above or below 100%?
4. Is RBC still above 120%?
5. Has cession rate changed significantly?
6. Can investment income offset underwriting losses?

### 9.2 Red Flags
- Loss ratio consistently > 80%
- COR > 110% without adequate investment income
- RBC approaching or below 120%
- GWP declining > 10% without clear explanation
- Cession rate > 70% (excessive reinsurance reliance)
- Catastrophe loss significantly distorting results
- Abnormal IBNR accumulation

### 9.3 Positive Signals
- Consistent premium growth with controlled loss ratio
- Good diversification across lines of business
- Stable investment income stream
- RBC well above 120%
- Expansion into digital distribution channels
